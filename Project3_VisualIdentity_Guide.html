Visual Identity — Color

The Psychology of Color

Leaf Green  #4A7C3F
Primary brand color. Vitality & health. Use for all primary buttons, CTAs, active icons, and key UI elements. Not neon — biological reality.
Earthy Brown  #5C3D2E
Secondary color. Stability. Soil-to-table connection that grounds the experience. Use for headings, body text, and structural elements.
Harvest Gold  #C8A84B
Accent color. Sunlight & quality. Used sparingly for delight moments — loading rings, highlights, certifications. Never for backgrounds.
Cream White  #F5F0E8
Background tone. Warm and organic — never pure white. Creates a premium, paper-like feel that evokes natural materials.
✕
Urgent Reds ✕ Anti-Pattern
Avoid completely. Triggers fast-food panic and destroys the premium organic feel. No warm reds, orange-reds, or tomato tones in primary UI.
Visual Identity — Typography

Typography & Voice

Editorial Authority
Playfair Display
Headline Font · Serif
Old World Heritage. High contrast and refined curves signal that content is curated, not just listed. Use for: screen titles, product names, hero headings, pull quotes.
Digital Utility
Montserrat
Body Font · Sans-Serif
New World Utility. Geometric and scalable. Ensures friction-free reading of nutrition data and pricing. Use for: all UI copy, prices, labels, body text, navigation.

Structural Foundation

The 8-Point Grid

The Rule: All dimensions, margins, and padding must be multiples of 8. This ensures pixel-perfect scaling across iPhone/Android densities (@1x, @2x, @3x) and creates a subconscious professional rhythm.

8px 16px 24px 32px 48px 64px

In Figma: set grid columns to 4 with 16px gutters and 16px margins. Every element's width, height, and spacing must snap to one of the values above.

Imagery Direction

North Window Light

✓ Do — Authentic & Raw
  • Natural daylight, soft shadows
  • Visible soil, textures, imperfections
  • Matte finishes — no gloss
  • Wooden surfaces, linen backgrounds
  • Produce that looks like it just left the farm
✕ Don't — Synthetic & Plastic
  • Oversaturated studio flash photography
  • Floating / cut-out ingredients on white
  • Too-perfect, processed-looking produce
  • Stock photo aesthetic — generic and lifeless
  • Harsh lighting with heavy shadows
Accessibility

Inclusivity is Hospitality

A
Contrast Ratio — minimum 4.5:1 (WCAG AA)
Leaf Green text on Cream White achieves ~7.2:1. Always verify with Figma's built-in contrast checker or plugins like "Contrast" or "Able". Headline text: aim for 15:1+.
B
Never use color alone to convey information
Error states must combine: red border + error icon + error text. A colorblind user must still understand the UI without color cues.
C
Touch targets — minimum 44×44px
All tappable elements (buttons, icons, cards) must be at least 44×44px per Apple HIG / Google Material guidelines. This is enforced by the 8-point grid.
✕
Anti-Patterns to Avoid No urgent reds · No neon greens · No cluttered cards · No studio-flash imagery · No floating cut-out produce · No generic purple-gradient AI aesthetics · No misaligned spacing (must be multiples of 8)
